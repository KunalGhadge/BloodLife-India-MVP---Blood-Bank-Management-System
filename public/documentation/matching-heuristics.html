06. The Blood Matching System

The "heart" of BloodLife is its automatic matching system. When someone needs blood, the system quickly scans all available donors to find a life-saving match based on medical compatibility.

Medical Fact: Not every blood type can donate to every other type. Our MatchFinder logic follows strict ABO and Rh-factor compatibility rules.

Compatibility Rules

Our algorithm follows standard medical guidelines:

  • The Universal Donor: O Negative (O-) can give blood to anyone.
  • The Universal Recipient: AB Positive (AB+) can receive blood from anyone.
  • Standard Matching: A+ can receive from A+, A-, O+, and O-.

How the Matching Works

When you view a blood request, the system runs these checks:

  1. Blood Group Compatibility: Does the donor's type match the recipient's type requirements?
  2. Eligibility Check: Has the donor donated too recently? (Usually, a 3-month gap is required).
  3. Availability: Is the donor currently available and reachable in the requested location?

Human Oversight

While the system suggests the best matches, the final decision always rests with a medical professional. The system is designed to assist, not replace, clinical judgment.
