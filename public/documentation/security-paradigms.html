15. Keeping Donor Data Secure

Security and Privacy are or top priorities. Since BloodLife deals with humanitarian data, we use several "Zero-Trust" strategies to ensure information remains in the right hands.

Our approach: Privacy by Design. We build security into the bedrock of the app rather than adding it as an afterthought.

Local-First Security

The best way to protect data is to not store it on an unsecured server. By keeping donor records on *your* device, we eliminate the risk of large-scale data breaches at a central location.

Sanitizing Input

We use strict "Escaping" rules to prevent malicious scripts from being run within the app (XSS protection). Every piece of text entered by a user is treated as untrusted and "sanitized" before it's displayed on the screen.

The Minimalist Policy

We only collect the data we *need*. By avoiding unnecessary personal identifiers (like full government IDs), we reduce the risk to donors while still maintaining an effective matching system.
