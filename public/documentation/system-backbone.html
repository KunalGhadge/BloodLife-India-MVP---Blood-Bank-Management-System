29. The Brain of the App (State)

How does the app remember that you just added a donor without needing to refresh the whole screen? That’s where State Management comes in. We call it the "Single Source of Truth."

Tech Explained: The AppState. It's a single object that holds ALL the information currently being shown on the screen.

How "State" Works

  • Input: You fill out a donor form and click "Save."
  • Action: An action is triggered (like addDonor).
  • Update: The "State Object" is updated with the new donor.
  • Reaction: React sees that the state has changed and automatically "re-draws" the parts of the screen that need to show the new donor.

Centralized Control

Instead of every component having its own complex memory, we keep the important stuff (donors, requests, and stock) in a central hub. This prevents bugs like two different parts of the app showing different donor counts.

Persistence Link

Every time the state changes, we "sync" it to the local storage. This ensures that the state isn't just in the app's brain but is also safely written to the user's hard drive.
