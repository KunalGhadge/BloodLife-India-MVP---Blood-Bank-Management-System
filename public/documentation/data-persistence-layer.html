05. How We Save Your Data

One of BloodLife's unique features is its Local Persistence. Unlike traditional apps that send everything to a server, BloodLife saves your donors, blood requests, and matches directly on your device.

Technical term: localStorage. It's a key-value storage system built into every modern web browser.

Why Local Storage?

By using your browser's local storage, we achieve three things:

  • Privacy: Your data never leaves your computer unless you explicitly choose to export or sync it.
  • Speed: Saving and loading data happens almost instantly since there's no "server" to talk to.
  • Reliability: You can use the app without an internet connection once it's loaded.

Managing the Data Shards

We use a custom utility called localDb.ts to manage this storage. It handles:

  1. Saving (SetItem): Converts your donors and requests into a "String" format and stores them.
  2. Loading (GetItem): Reads the data back and turns it into clean TypeScript objects your app can use.

Safety First

While local storage is great, it's tied to your browser. If you clear your browser's cache or switch devices, the data will be gone. We recommend regularly exporting your data or using our upcoming cloud-sync feature for permanent safety.
