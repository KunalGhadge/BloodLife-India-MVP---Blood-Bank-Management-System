19. Making Data Easy to Understand

Numbers are great, but charts are better. We use the Recharts library to turn boring rows of data into beautiful, interactive visualizations that help blood bank managers make quick decisions.

Strategy: Visual Insights. Instead of reading a list of 50 donors to see which blood group is most common, you can see it in a split-second on a Pie Chart.

What We Visualize

  • Donor Distribution: A bar chart showing how many donors we have for each blood group (A+, B-, etc.).
  • Request Trends: A line graph tracking the number of blood requests over time, helping us predict future needs.
  • Inventory Health: Radial gauges that show if our current stock is "Critical," "Safe," or "Optimal."

Interactivity

Our charts aren't static images. You can hover over a bar to see exact numbers, or click on a legend to filter the data. This "deep dive" capability gives admins total control over their data.

Performance Optimized

Wait, do charts slow down the app? Nope! We use useMemo to ensure the data is only recalculated when it actually changes, keeping the charts buttery smooth.
