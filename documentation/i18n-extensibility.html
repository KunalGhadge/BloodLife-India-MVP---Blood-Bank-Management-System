26. Designing for a Global Audience (i18n)

While BloodLife focuses on India, our mission is universal. We have designed the app to be easily translatable into any language, ensuring it can help communities around the world.

Goal: Linguistic Inclusion. Language should never be a barrier to saving a life.

How We Prepare for Translations

  • Externalized Strings: We avoid hard-coding text like "Save Donor" directly in the components. Instead, we use a central file where every piece of text is assigned a unique key.
  • Dynamic Direction: The layout is designed to support both Left-to-Right (LTR) languages like Hindi and English, and Right-to-Left (RTL) languages like Arabic or Urdu.
  • Number Formatting: We use standardized libraries to ensure that dates, times, and numbers are displayed in the format that feels natural to the user's region.

Our Plan for Indian Languages

Our immediate roadmap includes adding full support for Hindi, Marathi, Bengali, and Tamil. We invite community contributors to help us translate the central language files into their native tongues.

Cultural Adaptation

Beyond just translating words, we are careful with colors and symbols. We ensure that our icons and design choices are culturally sensitive and universally understood.
