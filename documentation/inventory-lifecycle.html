07. Managing the Blood Inventory

Keeping track of blood units is critical for any blood bank. BloodLife provides a detailed inventory management system to track every donation from the moment it's received until it's used.

Important Metric: Shelf Life. Blood is a biological resource and has a limited lifespan (usually around 35-42 days for red blood cells).

Tracking the Lifecycle

Every blood unit in the inventory has a "Lifecycle":

  • Available: The unit is fresh and ready to be used.
  • Reserved: The unit has been assigned to a specific request.
  • Used: The unit has been successfully transfused.
  • Expired: The unit has passed its safety date and should be safely discarded.

Dashboard Visibility

The main dashboard provides a "Stock Level" chart. This allows admins to see at a glance which blood groups are running low, ensuring they can proactively reach out to donors before an emergency occurs.

Safety Notifications

The system automatically flags units that are nearing their expiration date, helping blood bank managers prioritize older units first (FIFO - First In, First Out logic).
